# Executive Discovery Activities

Interactive activities for business executives that surface the **Discover** challenge — the friction of finding the right information, skill, or insight across distributed tools and data.

Built as standalone HTML pages, no login or setup required. Open any link and start immediately.

## Activity Summaries

### 1 — Paradox of Distributed Knowledge
Participants explore three simulated artifacts — an engineering Wiki, a GitHub repository (with clickable code files), and a Google Doc — to find answers to questions that require reading across all three. Surfaces the real cost of knowledge fragmentation.

**The challenge:** No single artifact has the full picture. Finding who owns a critical API endpoint, and how long a new user waits for access, requires piecing together information from different tools.

### 2 — Skills Compass
Participants are shown a catalog of 10 AI skills with brief descriptions, then challenged to sequence the right 2–3 skills to complete a specific workflow. Two scenarios of increasing complexity.

**The challenge:** With 10 options, identifying the exact right combination — and the correct order — requires careful reading. Mirrors the real difficulty of tool discovery in large organisations.

### 3 — Data Insights Challenge
Participants are given three business data tables (quarterly revenue, support metrics, headcount) and must answer six questions phrased in natural language — ranging from simple lookups to multi-table cross-referencing and ratio calculations.

**The challenge:** Each question requires scanning, calculating, or synthesising across tables. Highlights how much faster these insights could surface with an AI assistant that understands plain English.

---

## The Discover Theme

Each activity is designed around a single pain point: **Discover** — the difficulty of finding the right information, tool, or insight when knowledge is spread across systems, formats, and teams.

After each activity, a debrief prompt asks participants to connect the experience back to their own organisation: *How much of your day is spent discovering rather than deciding?*

---

## Using at a Conference

Each activity is a single self-contained HTML file. No internet connection required after the page loads (icons load from CDN on first open).

**Recommended setup:**
- Display the table above on a slide and ask participants to open activities on their laptops or phones
- Run as a timed group exercise (5–8 minutes per activity)
- Use the debrief prompt at the bottom of each activity to open a group discussion
